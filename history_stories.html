Where Heroes Rest, History Lives

Exploring the shared legacy of Indian and Canadian troops during World War I.

History of the Conflict

Second Battle of Ypres (1915)

Indian Lahore Division helped Canadian troops withstand the first German gas attack.

Battle of the Somme (1916)

Indian artillery supported Canadian forces in one of the war’s deadliest battles.

Battle of Vimy Ridge (1917)

Indian regiments assisted Canadian forces in this historic victory.

Key Historic Events

1914

Indian Lahore Division arrives in France, aiding exhausted British forces.

1915

Indians and Canadians fight together at Festubert and Passchendaele.

1918

The Battle of Haifa, led by Indian cavalry, secures a key city from Ottoman control.

Siege of Kut

Indian soldiers endured extreme hardships in Mesopotamia.

Personal Stories from Soldiers

"This is not war. It is the ending of the world."

– A Rajput soldier’s words from the trenches.

"God have mercy. The Germans are very strong, but as soon as the Indian troops arrived, their strength was broken."

– A Punjabi soldier.

"When we met the enemy hand to hand, my heart exulted."

– A Sikh soldier at Neuve Chapelle.

Letters from Soldiers
